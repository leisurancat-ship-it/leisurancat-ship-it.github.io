Kung Pao Chicken (宫保鸡丁)

A detailed, easy-to-follow recipe for the classic Sichuan-style stir-fry — balanced sweet, savory, spicy and tangy.

Serves: 2–3
Prep: 20 min
Cook: 10–12 min

Ingredients

For the chicken marinade

  • 300 g (about 10 oz) boneless chicken breast or thigh, diced into 1.5 cm cubes
  • 1 tsp light soy sauce
  • 1 tsp Shaoxing wine (or dry sherry)
  • 1 tsp cornstarch
  • ½ tsp salt

For the sauce & others

  • 1½ tbsp light soy sauce
  • 1 tsp dark soy sauce (optional, for color)
  • 1 tbsp Chinese black vinegar (or rice vinegar)
  • 1 tbsp Shaoxing wine
  • 1½ tsp sugar
  • 1 tsp cornstarch
  • 2 tbsp water
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil (peanut oil preferred)
  • 6–8 dried red chili peppers, halved, seeds removed for less heat
  • 1 tsp Sichuan peppercorns (optional)
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp fresh ginger, minced
  • 3–4 scallions, chopped into 2 cm pieces
  • ½ cup roasted peanuts

Step-by-step Instructions

  1. Marinate the chicken: In a bowl, combine diced chicken with light soy sauce, Shaoxing wine, cornstarch and salt. Mix well and let sit for 15–20 minutes to tenderize.
  2. Prepare the sauce: In a small bowl, whisk together light soy sauce, dark soy sauce (if using), black vinegar, Shaoxing wine, sugar, cornstarch and water until dissolved. Set aside.
  3. Heat the oil and aromatics: Warm the oil in a wok or large skillet over medium heat. Add dried chilies and Sichuan peppercorns and stir quickly for 20–30 seconds until fragrant — be careful not to burn them.
  4. Stir-fry the chicken: Raise heat to high, add marinated chicken and stir-fry until the outside turns white and pieces are nearly cooked through (about 3–4 minutes).
  5. Add garlic, ginger and scallions: Push the chicken to the side or make space in the wok, add minced garlic and ginger, stir briefly until fragrant, then combine with the chicken.
  6. Pour in the sauce: Add the prepared sauce and stir continuously until it thickens and coats the chicken evenly (about 1–2 minutes).
  7. Toss in peanuts and finish: Stir in roasted peanuts quickly, combine thoroughly, then remove from heat. Taste and adjust seasoning if needed.
  8. Serve: Transfer to a serving plate and enjoy immediately with steamed rice.

Tips & Variations

  • Adjust the number of dried chilies based on your spice tolerance.
  • Chicken thighs remain juicier than breasts and are recommended for more tender results.
  • For authentic flavor, use Chinese black vinegar instead of regular rice vinegar.
  • If you prefer, substitute cashews for peanuts to change the texture and flavor profile.
  • To make the dish gluten-free: use tamari (gluten-free soy sauce) and check Shaoxing wine alternatives or omit it.
